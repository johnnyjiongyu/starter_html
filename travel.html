Grand Canyon, Arizona, USA

The Grand Canyon is one of the most iconic and breathtaking natural wonders on Earth. It is known for its immense size, stunning geological formations, and dramatic landscapes. The Grand Canyon is considered one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

Temperatures at the Grand Canyon can vary greatly depending on the season and location within the park. Summers can be extremely hot, while winters can bring heavy snowfall.

Eiffel Tower, Paris, France

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. It was designed by the French engineer Gustave Eiffel and his construction company for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) held in Paris to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.

Its influence is huge around the world. There are numerous replicas of the Eiffel Tower around the world, including ones in Las Vegas, Nevada, and Tokyo, Japan.

Great Barrier Reef, Australia

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system, stretching over 2,300 kilometers (1,430 miles) along the northeast coast of Australia.

In addition, the reef is not only a natural wonder but also a critical location for scientific research. Scientists study the reef's biodiversity, climate change impacts, and various other aspects of marine ecology.

The Great Wall of China

The construction of the wall began over 2,000 years ago, with various dynasties contributing to its expansion and reinforcement over the centuries. It stretches over approximately 21,196 kilometers (13,171 miles).

In addition to its military and defensive purposes, the Great Wall also played a crucial role in trade, cultural exchange, and border control.

Colosseum, Rome, Italy

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient Roman amphitheater located in the center of Rome. The construction of the Colosseum began in AD 72 under the emperor Vespasian and was completed in AD 80 under his successor and heir, Titus.

In recent centuries, significant restoration work has been undertaken to preserve and protect the Colosseum. It is now a major tourist attraction in Rome.

Big Ben, London, UK

Big Ben is actually the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London. The official name of the tower is the Elizabeth Tower.

The clock mechanism was designed to be incredibly accurate and reliable. Even today, the clock's accuracy is maintained through regular adjustments.
